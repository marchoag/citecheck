How CiteCheck handles your data

Last Updated: June 10, 2025

What We Collect

API Keys: We temporarily store your CourtListener API key in your browser's memory only during your session. This key is never saved to our servers, databases, or any persistent storage.

Search Queries: We process your citation search text to query the CourtListener database, but we do not store your searches.

Technical Data: Standard web server logs (IP address, browser type, timestamps) for security and performance monitoring.

Bug Reports: When you submit bug reports through our form, we collect the issue description and optional email address you provide. This data is sent directly to our email for service improvement purposes.

  • ‚ùå Your search history
  • ‚ùå Personal information
  • ‚ùå Cookies or tracking data
  • ‚ùå Legal documents or case content

Third-Party Services

Render: Our hosting provider. Review their Privacy Policy.

Your Rights

Since we don't store personal data, there's nothing to delete or modify. Your API key stays in your browser only and is automatically cleared when you leave.

Security

We use HTTPS encryption and follow security best practices. Your API key never leaves your browser except to make authorized requests to CourtListener.

Questions about privacy? Contact us through our GitHub repository.
